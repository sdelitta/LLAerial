Hen Island, NY

Hen Island got its name during the Revolutionary War. Legend has it, a local farmer (fearing that his chickens would be ripped off by the marauding bands) stashed away his flock on the little-noticed island between the Mamaroneck and Rye Harbors. There they remained for the duration of the war and from this the island derived its name: Hen Island.
